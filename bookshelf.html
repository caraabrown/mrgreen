The Bookshelf. In His Words.

These are not assigned readings. They are personal recommendations — the books Mr. Green returns to when someone says "I want to understand this better." Each entry will carry his own short take: why this book, why now, why it matters to the world you're living in today.

Mr. Green's personal commentary on each book is coming soon — this page will be updated in his own words.

The Spook Who Sat by the Door
Sam Greenlee
01
The Spook Who Sat by the Door
Sam Greenlee · 1969

“This is the founding philosophical text of the O'Dowd BSU. Greenlee's message is simple and radical: sit by the door, let them see you, and execute your goals from inside the system. Mr. Green assigned this book the first year the BSU existed.”

Fiction
The Autobiography of Malcolm X
Alex Haley
02
The Autobiography of Malcolm X
Alex Haley · 1965

“No book has explained the through-line from slavery to the present more clearly than Malcolm X's own words. Read this before anything else — it gives you the lens.”

Biography
Miseducation of the Negro
Carter G. Woodson
03
Miseducation of the Negro
Carter G. Woodson · 1933

“Woodson wrote this in 1933 and it describes 2024. If you control what a man thinks, you never have to worry about what he does. Start here.”

Black History
Invisible Man
Ralph Ellison
04
Invisible Man
Ralph Ellison · 1952

“The most honest novel ever written about what it means to be Black in America — the invisibility that comes not from hiding, but from being deliberately unseen. Ellison said what couldn't be said any other way.”

Fiction
Race Matters
Cornel West
05
Race Matters
Cornel West · 1993

“West draws the line between individual prejudice and systemic power more clearly than almost anyone. This is the distinction that changes how you see everything.”

Politics & Power
Up From Slavery
Booker T. Washington
06
Up From Slavery
Booker T. Washington · 1901

“Washington's story is inseparable from the argument about how Black Americans should navigate a system designed against them. Read it alongside Du Bois. The debate between them is still happening.”

Biography
Malcolm X: A Life of Reinvention
Manning Marable
07
Malcolm X: A Life of Reinvention
Manning Marable · 2011

“Marable gives you the man behind the myth — the evolving, questioning, growing Malcolm who was more complex and more radical than either his critics or his admirers understood.”

Biography

08
The Poisonwood Bible
Barbara Kingsolver · 1998

“A white American family goes to the Congo to save it and discovers they need saving themselves. The best novel about colonialism you will ever read — and it will change how you see American foreign policy.”

Wealth of Nations
