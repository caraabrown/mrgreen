The Man Behind The Platform

Mr. Anthony Green

History Instructor · BSU Moderator · Coach · Bishop O'Dowd High School

There is a particular kind of teacher who doesn't just teach a subject — they teach students how to think about the world they live in. Mr. Anthony Green is that teacher. Since 1986, he has stood in the same institution, in front of thousands of students from over 100 communities across the Oakland Bay Area, and done something quietly extraordinary: he has told the full story of America.

Mr. Green helped co-found what is recognized as the oldest continuously running Black Student Union in the nation at Bishop O'Dowd High School — a living institution that has hosted some of the most significant civil rights voices of the last four decades, including figures from the civil rights movement, Black Power era, and contemporary activism.

He developed and leads O'Dowd's Advanced Placement African American Studies curriculum — one of the few programs of its kind at any high school in the country. His classes don't happen only in a classroom. They happen at the Black Panthers museum in West Oakland. At Marcus Bookstore — the oldest Black bookstore in America. At Lois The Pie Queen. History you can walk through, not just read about.

He is not an activist. He is a teacher. And after 37 years of getting the facts right, his record speaks with an authority that no single opinion can touch.

A Living Legacy

The Oldest Running Black
Student Union in America

Mr. Green helped co-found the Bishop O'Dowd Black Student Union — and it never stopped running. Decades later, it remains an active, student-led institution that has brought civil rights icons, scholars, activists, and community leaders into a high school auditorium in Oakland, CA. What began as an act of advocacy became a permanent feature of one of California's most respected academic institutions. That is not an accident. That is the result of one person who believed students deserved access to their full history — and built something to give it to them.
